Initial Discussion: Industry 4.0, with its integration of IoT, AI, and big data, is transforming healthcare by enhancing Electronic Patient Records (EPR), diagnostics, and personalised treatment. However, these advancements also increase the sector’s vulnerability to information system failures (Schwab, 2016). A significant example is the NHS ransomware attack in 2017, known as WannaCry, which severely disrupted healthcare services across the UK. This cyber-attack exploited outdated NHS systems, locking critical medical data and forcing the cancellation of numerous appointments and surgeries, thereby jeopardising patient safety and care continuity. The impact was substantial: with restricted access to EPRs, clinicians struggled to make timely decisions, highlighting the essential role of secure, accessible data in healthcare. Economically, the attack led to millions in losses for the NHS due to both immediate disruption and long-term recovery efforts. The reputational damage also influenced public trust, raising concerns about the NHS’s ability to protect sensitive information (NHS England, 2023). In summary, this incident demonstrates the urgent need for robust cybersecurity in the face of Industry 4.0 advancements.
