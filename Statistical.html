Reflection on my learning

Working through Units 7 & 8 sharpened both my statistical judgment and my digital‑toolkit.

Statistically, I realised how assumption checks drive trustworthy inference: the F‑test on diet variances and the normality check on paired differences both determined which t-test version I could defend. Translating p-values into plain language for a business audience was trickier than the calculation itself, and showed me communication is half the battle.

Technically, I became fluent with Excel’s Data Analysis ToolPak, switching from hand‑built formulas to automated outputs, and learnt to create accessible visuals (box plots, bar charts) complete with alt‑text. Embedding files and links in HTML taught me the nuts and bolts of reproducible reporting.

Next step? Re‑run the analyses in R to script the workflow and add diagnostic plots, cementing the habit of reproducibility I’ve started here.
